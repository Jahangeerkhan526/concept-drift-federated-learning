01
What is Federated Learning?

Federated Learning (FL) allows multiple organisations to train a shared AI model together — without sharing their raw data. Each organisation trains locally on their own data and only sends model weights (patterns) to a central server.

02
The Two Problems
🗂️
Non-IID Data
Each organisation always has different data. Bank A has rich customers, Bank B has students. This is permanent and expected — it never changes.
🌊
Concept Drift
An organisation's data suddenly changes over time. Fraud patterns evolve. New virus appears. This is unexpected and causes the model to fail.
03
The Research Gap

When the server receives a bad update from a client, it cannot tell whether:

❓
Non-IID?
This client was always different — it is just unique. Normal! Ignore it.
⚠️
Concept Drift?
This client's data just changed — something happened! Act now!
"Existing research addresses Non-IID and Concept Drift separately. No work uses a client's own gradient history to distinguish between the two."
04
Research Question
"Can cosine similarity detect concept drift in a Non-IID Federated Learning environment?"
05
